# Cognitive Science

Cognitive science studies the computational nature of mind and intelligence — how perception, memory, language, reasoning, and consciousness are realized in biological and artificial systems. It is the "understanding the mind" layer of brain-like intelligence, bridging neuroscience (mechanism) and AI (implementation).

## Chapter Outline

1. **Foundations of Cognitive Science** — History, paradigms, and methodology of cognitive science.
2. **Perception & Attention** — From sensory input to perceptual construction, and the selection and limits of attention.
3. **Memory & Learning** — Multiple memory systems and the cognitive mechanisms of learning.
4. **Language & Symbols** — Language as a window on cognition: from phonology to semantics to pragmatics.
5. **Reasoning & Decision-Making** — How people infer, judge, and choose under uncertainty.
6. **Concepts & Categories** — How knowledge is organized into concepts and categories.
7. **Consciousness & Self** — Subjective experience, self-awareness, and the "hard problem" of mind.
8. **Computational Models of Cognition** — Capturing cognition with runnable computational models.
